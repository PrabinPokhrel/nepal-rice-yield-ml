EXPLORATORY FINDINGS - figure01

Rice yield trend: +33.3 kg/ha per year (r²=0.837, p<0.001)
Temperature trend: +0.0206°C per year

Correlations with rice yield:
  tasmin (min temp): r=0.761  STRONGEST
  tas_mean:          r=0.679
  tasmax:            r=0.624

Key observation: minimum temperature has strongest correlation.
This matches Bangladesh paper (Roy and Uddin 2025).
But mechanism may differ for Nepal:
  - Bangladesh: warmer nights HURT yield (grain filling stress)
  - Nepal: warmer nights may HELP yield (longer growing season,
    less frost at altitude, earlier planting)
This needs discussion in paper - important original insight.

The colour pattern in scatter plots (red=old, green=recent):
  - Old years (red): low temp, low yield - bottom left
  - Recent years (green): high temp, high yield - top right
  - Both yield and temperature increased together over time
  - This means r values partly reflect the shared TIME trend
    not purely a direct temperature-yield relationship
  - This is called CONFOUNDING by time trend
  - ML models can handle this better than simple correlation

Figure 02: Rainfall findings

Rainfall correlation with yield: r=0.259 (weak but positive)
This is DIFFERENT from Bangladesh paper (negative/non-significant)

Reason for Nepal being positive:
- Nepal is mostly rain-fed agriculture (no major irrigation)
- Terai plains depend on monsoon water directly
- Mountain terrain drains excess water faster than flat Bangladesh
- So more rain = more water = slightly higher yield in Nepal

Key ranking of variables:
1. tasmin (min temp):  r=0.761  strongest predictor
2. tas_mean:           r=0.679
3. tasmax:             r=0.624
4. rainfall:           r=0.259  weakest

Rainfall trend: +0.8 mm/year (p=0.596, NOT significant)
Rainfall is NOT changing significantly over time.
Temperature IS changing significantly.
This means climate change is affecting Nepal rice
primarily through temperature, not rainfall changes.

CRITICAL FINDING - Spurious correlation explained:

tasmin r=0.761 with yield BUT only 3.0% importance detrended.
Why: both tasmin and yield increased over time (shared trend).
This is spurious correlation, not direct causation.

Correct interpretation:
- Long-term warming trend correlates with yield increase
- But year-to-year temperature variation does NOT predict
  year-to-year yield variation
- Rainfall (14.4%) and fertilizer (48.5%) drive annual variation
- Technology adoption drives the long-term trend

This distinction is critical for the paper.
Must not overstate temperature-yield relationship.
Panel E interpretation (my own words):
Phosphate fertilizer is most important for year-to-year variation.
Rainfall is most important climate variable.
Temperature variables are less important than rainfall.
Humidity is least important.

This means: in years when phosphate supply was good AND
rainfall was adequate, yield was above trend.
In years when phosphate was scarce OR rainfall was poor,
yield fell below trend.
